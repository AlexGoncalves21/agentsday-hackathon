# Demis Hassabis

## Information

Demis Hassabis is a British AI researcher, entrepreneur, and the co-founder and CEO of Google DeepMind. DeepMind was founded in London in 2010 and acquired by Google in 2014. In 2023, Google combined DeepMind and Google Brain into Google DeepMind, with Hassabis as CEO. The organization is one of the most important AI research labs in the world and is associated with reinforcement learning, game-playing systems, scientific AI, and frontier model development.

Hassabis' career connects games, neuroscience, artificial intelligence, and scientific discovery. Before DeepMind, he worked in games and co-designed Theme Park early in his career. He later studied cognitive neuroscience, and DeepMind's early identity combined ideas from neuroscience, machine learning, and reinforcement learning. DeepMind's AlphaGo victory over Lee Sedol in 2016 became a cultural milestone for AI because Go had long been considered too complex for brute-force search alone.

Hassabis shared the 2024 Nobel Prize in Chemistry with David Baker and John Jumper. Hassabis and Jumper were recognized for protein structure prediction work connected to AlphaFold, while Baker was recognized for computational protein design. AlphaFold demonstrated that AI could become a scientific discovery engine, not merely a tool for chat or automation. The Nobel recognition made DeepMind's scientific impact visible far beyond the AI community.

Important themes around Hassabis:

- AI as a general-purpose scientific discovery tool.
- Reinforcement learning and agents as a route toward more general intelligence.
- Games as test environments for planning, search, and learning.
- Protein folding as a breakthrough example of AI applied to biology.
- The shift from AI demos to AI systems that generate new scientific knowledge.
- "Learning how to learn" as a core skill in an AI-shaped world.

Connections for the second brain include AlphaGo, AlphaFold, Google DeepMind, reinforcement learning, agent design, AI for science, AGI, game theory, decision theory, and the use of games as controlled environments for intelligence research.
