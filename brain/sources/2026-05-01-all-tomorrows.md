## Information

All Tomorrows is a speculative evolution and science fiction work by Turkish artist and author C. M. Kosemen. Its full title is commonly given as "All Tomorrows: A Billion Year Chronicle of the Myriad Species and Mixed Fortunes of Man." It was originally published online in 2006 and became widely discussed through internet communities, YouTube videos, fan art, memes, and speculative biology circles.

The work imagines humanity across enormous future timescales. Humans spread through the galaxy, encounter the Qu, and are radically altered into many posthuman species. The format is pseudo-historical rather than a conventional novel: it reads like a distant chronicle or catalog of future species, civilizations, catastrophes, adaptations, and transformations.

Important themes:

- speculative evolution
- posthumanism
- deep time
- body horror
- artificial selection
- civilizational fragility
- cosmic scale
- biological imagination
- worldbuilding through taxonomy
- loss, adaptation, and transformation

The Qu are central antagonistic forces. They reshape human descendants into strange forms, often as punishment, experiment, or domination. The book's emotional force comes partly from its scale: individual human concerns disappear into evolutionary and cosmic history, yet traces of identity, suffering, adaptation, and dignity remain.
